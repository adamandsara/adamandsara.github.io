Speeches

Swedish Wedding Speeches - What's All This Then? If you're Irish, you might be expecting a few speeches at the end of the meal-the best man, maybe the father of the bride, a few heartfelt words and a toast or two. But this is also a Swedish wedding... and here, speeches are a whole thing. At Swedish weddings, speeches happen throughout the meal, not just at the end. Friends, family, distant relatives, even co-workers might get up and say a few words. Some will be funny, some touching, some musical-you never quite know what's coming. And that's part of the fun! Here's what to expect:
  • They're spread out: Speeches are spaced between courses, so the meal keeps flowing.
  • Anyone can speak (who signs up in advance): There's usually a speech coordinator, so things don't get out of hand.
  • They're personal: Expect stories, songs, props, and maybe the occasional tear.
  • You can join in! If you're inspired to say a few words, ask the toastmaster-it's not too late.
So sit back, enjoy the show, and get ready for some very Swedish-style storytelling. Sláinte and skål!

Breakfast, lunch and dinner

If you choose to arrive at the Friday, you are invited to have pizza with us at Rock Farm!

Breakfast can be bought when booking the accommodation. There is also the possibility to make your own breakfast in the kitchens at the Rock Farm area, if you bring ingredients. We will also put some ingredients there for a basic breakfast.

Lunch can be bought in the village of Slane, or you can prepare your own lunch in the kitchens at the area, if you bring ingredients.
